Our Mission 
Young Hearts Old Souls is dedicated to fostering meaningful connections between youth and senior citizens, celebrating the diversity and wisdom of older generations. Our mission is to build an inclusive community that empowers the elderly and enriches the lives of young people.
Supporting Seniors' Integration into Canadian Society
At Young Hearts Old Souls, we are dedicated to fostering meaningful connections between local youth and elders, including seniors from diverse cultural backgrounds who are new to Canada. By bridging the gap between generations, we aim to support the integration of these seniors into the fabric of Canadian society.
Through our programs and initiatives, we create opportunities for meaningful interactions, shared experiences, and the exchange of wisdom and perspectives. This helps seniors, particularly those new to Canada, feel a greater sense of belonging and connection to their local communities.
Cultivate Compassionate and Inclusive Communities
  • Foster a spirit of empathy and understanding between youth and seniors from diverse backgrounds.
  • Organize multicultural events and intergenerational activities that celebrate the richness of Canada's cultural tapestry.
  • Encourage mutual respect and open communication to break down stereotypes and build lasting friendships.
Enhance Elderly Persons' Wellbeing
By fostering meaningful connections, Young Hearts Old Souls aims to enrich the lives of senior citizens. Our programs focus on boosting their sense of belonging, happiness, and overall health.
  1. Create opportunities for social engagement to combat isolation and loneliness.
  1. Organize wellness activities that promote physical, mental, and emotional wellbeing.
  1. Celebrate the wisdom and life experiences of the elderly, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

Our Programs
At Young Hearts Old Souls, we offer a range of supportive programs tailored to the needs of seniors. From volunteer activities and community events to engaging workshops and clubs, we strive to create meaningful opportunities for the elderly to stay active, learn new skills, and forge connections within the community.
By organizing these diverse programs, we aim to enhance the overall well-being and quality of life for the elderly, fostering a sense of purpose, belonging, and engagement.
Collaborative Partnerships for Seniors' Engagement
  1. Forge strategic alliances with community centers, retirement homes, and cultural organizations to coordinate shared programming and resources.
  1. Collaborate with local government agencies to advocate for policies and initiatives that support the integration and wellbeing of senior citizens.
  1. Partner with educational institutions to develop intergenerational mentorship programs, where students can learn from the wisdom and life experiences of elderly participants.
